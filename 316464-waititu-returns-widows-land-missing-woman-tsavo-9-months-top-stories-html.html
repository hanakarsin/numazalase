Waititu returns widow's land, missing woman found in Tsavo after 9 months and all top stories

July 2024 · 3 minute read

Thursday, September 26 is at homestretch and here is a roundup of the stories that have made headlines today.

1. Chuka woman who went missing nearly a year ago found at Tsavo National park

A woman and her daughter from Chuka in Tharaka Nithi county have been reunited nine months after the former went missing and later found inside a national park.

Police suppose she could have walked from Chuka to Tsavo West National park

2. Isaac Mwaura called out for using photo of a Malawian child as his TBT

Nominated Senator Isaac Mwaura was mocked online for posting a photo of a Malawian child and claiming he was the one in the photo.

The photo was taken by a media company which wanted to highlight the plight of children living with albinism.

3. Isiolo court workers and police officers raise KSh 11k to bail out single father of three

Police officers and court workers at the Isiolo Law Courts came together and contributed their own money to bail out a poor father of three who was arrested and was about to be jailed for selling chips in unhygienic conditions.

The father has been selling the fries to provide for his family.

4. Governor Ferdinand Waititu admits to having grabbed KSh 100 million land belonging to widow

Embattled Kiambu county Governor Ferdinand Waititu confessed to illegally acquiring land worth KSh 100 million belonging to a widow.

The prime property is reported to belong to Cecilia Mbugua but was fraudulently transferred to Esther Nyatu through the office of the governor and the governor himself.

5. Gloria Muliro's Ex-husband Eric Ombewa accuses the singer's friends of their divorce

After being silent for ages, the singer’s ex-hubby Eric Omba accused the beauty’s friends of meddling in their relationship.

He said Gloria's friends misguided her with wrong advice.

6. Census workers decry lack of payment and missing of names from the payment list

The officials accused the government of subjecting them to tough times despite their devotion to work for long hours to ensure the complex exercise became successful.

Some officials also accused county officials of blotting out their names from the payment list and inserting names of people who did not work.
