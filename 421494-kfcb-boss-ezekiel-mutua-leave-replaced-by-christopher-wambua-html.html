KFCB Boss Ezekiel Mutua Sent on Terminal Leave, Christopher Wambua Named Acting CEO

March 2024 · 3 minute read
  • The KFCB CEO Ezekiel Mutua was on Friday, August 6, asked to proceed on terminal leave
  • He was replaced by Christopher Wambua from the Communications Authority of Kenya in an acting capacity
  • Mutua is expected to be on terminal leave until the expiry of his term which was due on October 25
  • The KFCB boss had on Thursday, August 5, denied reports that he had been fired
  • He called the reports malicious and said he was not aware of them

Kenya Films and Classification Board (KFCB) CEO Ezekiel Mutua has been asked to proceed on terminal leave, until the expiry of his term.

Christopher Wambua from the Communications Authority of Kenya (CAK) was appointed to replace him in an acting capacity, for a period of two months.

Mutua's second term contract is due to expire on October 25.

The new development came hours after the moral police denied reports that he had been fired calling the reports “malicious”. He said he was not aware of them.

“My attention has been drawn to reports on social media to the effect that I have been fired as CEO of KFCB. Please ignore such malicious rumours," he tweeted on Thursday.

"I am not aware of such developments and there can be no grain of truth in them as there’s no vacancy in the office of the CEO KFCB.”

Ezekiel Mutua's woes

Mutua was appointed to the role for the first time in October 2015 and was reappointed three years later.

His problems stem from a report submitted to the State Corporations Advisory Committee (SCAC) that the KFCB board of directors met on June 30 to discuss the CEO's contract renewal.

The board was also accused of interfering with the duties of an Inspector General of State Corporations by refusing him access to a board meeting.

This was in violation of Section (18) 2 of the State Corporation Act (Cap.446).

“The board of KFCB knowingly convened to deliberate and make a determination on a matter that is already predetermined as not feasible,” ICT CS Joe Mucheru said in a confidential letter.“Any decision made towards renewal of the CEO’s contract for a third term is illegal and void,” he further stated.
