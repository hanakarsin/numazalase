Video of Raila Limbering Vigorously as He Celebrates Kenyan Athletes at Kasarani Excites Netizens

February 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Former prime minister Raila Odinga graced the last day of the World Under-20 Athletics Championship held at Nairobi's Moi International Sports Center - Kasarani
  • He cheered the Kenyan team, which bagged a total of seven medals on the last day of the competition
  • Overall, Kenya secured 16 medals; eight gold, one silver and seven bronze, this being regarded as the best perfomance to have ever been posted at the WU20 championships by a Kenyan team

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader Raila Odinga was among the spectators during the final day of the World Under-20 Athletics Championships held at the Moi Kasarani Stadium, Nairobi.

Raila was thrilled by the ace performance posted by the Kenyan athletes who dominated the five-day event.

During the men's 800 metres, the ODM leader could not withstand the excitement when he left his seat to cheer on the Kenyan team, which almost surrendered to Algeria.

He is seen limbering energetically as Kenya's Emmanuel Wanyonyi struggles to make it to the finish line ahead of his competitors.

Wanyonyi managed to clinch the gold medal setting a new championship record of 1:43.76, Algeria securing silver.

The 18th edition of the WU20 fete saw Kenya finishing at the top of the medal standings with an overall tally of 16 medals.

During the final day on Sunday, August 22, Kenyan athletes bagged a total of seven medals; three gold, a silver and three bronze.

The gold medalists on the final day were Amos Serem, who won the men’s 3000 metres steeplechase, Emmanuel Wanyonyi who won the men's 800 metres, and Purity Chepkirui who emerged the fastest in the women's 1500 metres.

Bronze medalists were Simon Koech (men's 3000m steeplechase), Winnie Jemutai (women's 1500m) and Noah Kibet (men's 800m).

Overall, Kenya had eight gold, one silver and seven bronze, this being the best performance to be ever posted in 11 years by the country.

The country ended the championships as the most successful nation.

Medals aside, they also headed the placings table with 128 points from Ethiopia's 111 and Jamaica's 100.

Taking to his official Twitter handle, Raila hailed the team for their great performance since the beginning of the event last week.

"Congratulations to our Athletes on their sterling performance at the #WorldAthleticsU20 Championships successfully hosted in Kasarani. The Flag of Kenya team grabbed a massive haul that includes eight Gold Medals to emerge top of the table. Our youth have shown great promise. Well done!" he wrote.

The championships began on Wednesday, August 18, ending on Sunday, August 22.
