Factors to consider when choosing a nickname

In an interview with Legit.ng, Ademilola Adenekan-Emmanuel, a public health professional and child mentor, said that nicknames should be carefully chosen as they could cause the possibility of creating a long-lasting effect on an individual's life by how they perceive themselves or how others perceive them. She said:

I am a Christian and from a spiritual perspective, a name is a word constantly prophesied into a person's life every time it is mentioned. If it is a positive nickname, great. But if it is a negative one, it's like placing a curse on a person every time he/she is called. If the nickname is chosen during wonderful and positive circumstances that bring positive memories, then a nickname can be alright.For example when a nickname is formed during a mishap or embarrassing experience while the person is still young, e.g in school or at home among friends/family, it can cause the individual to suffer from low self-esteem for a very long time and when called that nickname, it is a constant reminder of that event. It becomes ingrained into the person's identity and they might even eventually give up and accept that stigma for life.

Ademilola concluded her insights by highlighting the role of cultural influences in shaping the selection of nicknames. Expanding on this, she explained that the choice of nicknames can also be influenced by individual characteristics and physical attributes. She emphasised that:

Some regions can have a mix of different cultures, so I prefer to stick with cultural influences since a person would normally be of one major culture. Based on gender roles dictated or imposed by specific cultures, it will determine what kind of nicknames females bear and males bear. Their social class can also have an influence on their nicknames. There are some names that cannot be heard among the rich and sophisticated due to exposure, but might be common among the illiterate or poorer social classes.A lot of nicknames are based on personal and physical attributes. And this is where they can become negative, because people evolve. When a person is trying to evolve from a specific initial state and is still called a nickname from the past, the person is constantly being reminded of his/her past, almost refusing them the opportunity to evolve and move on with their lives. This leads to self esteem issues or identity crisis in many people.
