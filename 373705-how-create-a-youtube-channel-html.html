How to create a YouTube channel (Step-by-step guide with pictures)

February 2024 · 8 minute read

Did you know that YouTube is the second most visited site in the world? Apart from being a popular source of free entertainment, the site is a haven for creators and other people who are interested in sharing knowledge. Starting out as a content creator on YouTube isn't hard. In fact, learning how to create a YouTube channel should only take a few minutes.

Is it free to create a YouTube channel? Yes. One of the main reasons why the site has been able to reach billions of people is its progressive policy regarding creators. Apart from allowing people to reach audiences through videos, YouTube has the best monetisation systems in the streaming world.

How to create a YouTube channel – a step-by-step guide

While the requirements for creating a channel have largely remained unchanged over the years, the process has undergone some adjustments. For example, you must be in the Google ecosystem to be part of YouTube. Follow these steps on how to start a YouTube channel for beginners:

Step 1: Create a Google account

Google is YouTube’s parent company. Therefore, you must create a Google account before you open your YouTube channel. However, if you have a Gmail account, you can skip to Step 2.

To create a Google account:

  • Go to the Google Accounts page
  • Click the “Create Account” option
  • Fill your details and select a password

The account you have created allows you to access all Google services such as YouTube and Gmail. At this point, you can use your YouTube account and upload videos. However, for visibility and monetisation, follow the subsequent steps.

Step 2: Customise your channel

Customisation helps your channel to stand out. If you used your real names to create a Google account, you need to create a separate channel to give your new brand autonomy. Depending on your needs, you can either use your name as a brand or pick your preferred brand name for your channel.

To customise your channel:

  • On your browser, click YouTube
  • Log in using your Google Account’s details
  • Go to the Channels page
  • Create a new brand’s channel

When creating a new channel, the name you choose should reflect what you are offering. However, you can still use your real names if you do not have a brand name.

Step 3: Polish-up your profile

Your channel’s icon, profile pic and banner players a huge role in viewership. Therefore, you must tweak these details and make your channel stand out from the rest. You can hire the services of a graphic designer to create a good logo and banner or you can do it yourself.

To polish-up your channel’s profile:

  • Go to YouTube
  • Tap your profile’s icon
  • Select “Your Channel” option
  • Click “Customise Channel”
  • Edit your channel’s icon by uploading a different picture
  • Change the “Channel Art” by uploading your preferred image

Step 4: Create a channel’s trailer

The trailer allows you to create a good impression of the channel. While you can use the trailer’s slot to highlight a new video, using it to introduce yourself to potential subscribers helps in growing your channel.

If you are uncertain about your creative direction as a YouTuber, using this slot to highlight your most recent project is advisable. Also, the channel’s trailer is not permanent. You can easily re-do it in future if you wish.

Step 5: Organise your channel

Once you have uploaded some videos, you should create the channel’s structure. While YouTube allows newer videos to show first, the structure may make it hard to navigate the channel, especially to potential subscribers.

To create YouTube playlists on your channel:

Different ways to monetise your YouTube channel

Apart from reaching to more people and growing a brand, you can start a YouTube channel and get paid. However, you need to grow your audience before you can start monetising. The three main ways to make money from a YouTube channel. You can use any or both, depending on your audience and preferences.

1. YouTube AdSense

While YouTube Originals is becoming popular, advertising is the biggest revenue for the site. The company pays content creators more than half of the money collected through AdSense. Therefore to earn from the platform, you must link your AdSense account to your channel.

Second, you must have over 1000 subscribers on your channel to earn from this source of revenue. Also, the minimum watch time is 4000 watch hours, regardless of how many videos are on your channel.

2. Sponsored content

Companies and brand may contact you for product placement. For example, if you are a beauty channel, a company making beauty products may use your channel to reach to clients. YouTube allows creators to work with brands as long as the product and services on the videos are legal.

3. Patreon

If you have a successful YouTube channel and you want a consistent monthly income, sign up on Patreon. Also, encourage your viewers to subscribe to your Patreon page for the exclusive content. Unlike YouTube, where the income is based on watch hours, the platform is based on members’ contributions.
