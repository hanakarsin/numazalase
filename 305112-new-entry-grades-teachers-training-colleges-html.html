New entry grades for teachers training colleges

March 2024 · 4 minute read

Thousands of students have been left disappointed after a notice was issued on the intention of the government to revoke low-grade admission. There is a likelihood for students who have been admitted with D plain and C minus being shown the door at the various public and private institutions in the country. The issue has been subjected to various court hearings, but as it is now, there may be no light at the end of the tunnel for the students involved. Faced with this dilemma, many are asking when the new entry grades for teachers training colleges will come to effect.

The decision to lower admission grades for P1 teachers training colleges in Kenya was applauded and opposed in equal measure by different factions in the country. The idea may have been well intended with the education ministry feeling that this was the best solution to breach the demand gap of tutors in the country. The idea came under heavy criticism that doing so could only dilute the education levels in the country because the minimum grades were not enough for one to become a teacher.

While the rest of the country was engaged in the pros and cons of low-grade admissions, the teacher training colleges in the country felt that the idea’s time had come. Thus they enrolled several students under the program. The notice that had been spearheaded by former education cabinet secretary Amina Mohamed but has now been quashed under the reign of Professor Magoha.

Legal notice

A letter sent by the education principal secretary, Mr. Belio Kipsang’, instructed all principals of teacher training colleges to adhere to Legal Notice No. 50 of 2016. This means that the reforms undertaken by Amina Mohamed would no longer be effected. Those students with a grade lower than C plain are advised to seek courses in technical training colleges.

New entry grades

During the tenure of CS Amina Mohamed, the minimum grades were set at D for certificate courses and C minus for diploma courses. The new entry grades for teachers training colleges will be C plain for certificate courses and C plus for diploma courses for all students intending to undergo teacher training courses. The grades will not only apply to public primary teachers training colleges in Kenya but also the private ones as well.

The way forward

It is clear that the move to reduce minimum entry grades for teacher training colleges was unconstitutional. Students who had been enrolled through the low-grade admission ought not to have been admitted in the first place. The best action plan under the circumstances would be to seek the guidance of their respective principals. Although, given the quashing of the previous notice students ought to be advised to seek alternative courses which they qualify. Some students might insist on completing their teacher training course but of what use will it be if they won’t be employed with the same qualifications?

Students who have been locked out by the new entry grades for teachers training colleges should know that this is not the end of the road. There are many other courses offered at technical institutions all over the country. Such courses should be considered and might turn out to be even more marketable. Those who have made the cut based on the new criteria should fill a teachers training college application form obtained from the relevant institution. Those confident that their grades are even higher can check if they qualify for secondary teachers training colleges in Kenya.
