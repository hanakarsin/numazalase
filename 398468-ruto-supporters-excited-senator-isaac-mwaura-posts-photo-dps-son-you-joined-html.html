Deputy President William Ruto's supporters are over the moon after Nominated Senator Isaac Mwaura attended the newly elected Msambweni MP Feisar Badar's homecoming ceremony.

Not only did Mwaura attend the event on Thursday, December 31, but he also took time to take pictures with Ruto's son Nick and Kikuyu MP Kimani Ichung'wa.

The senator late shared the photos online and Ruto's supporters warmly welcomed him to the hustler nation with many telling him he had made a wise decision.

Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro confirmed that indeed the lawmaker had joined the hustler nation.

"Welcome home, Senator Isaac Mwaura. Thank you for officially joining the Hustler Nation. Many more on the way. Thank you," Nyoro wrote.

As was earlier reported by TUKO.co,ke, The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) declared Bader the winner of the mini-poll with 15,251 votes against his close competitor ODM candidate Omar Boga's 10,444.

Bader had taken an early lead right from the beginning of the tallying of votes exercise carried out at Dr. Babla Secondary School.

Wiper's candidate came in third with 790 votes, followed by Hamisi Mwakonje with 230 then Marere Machai with 300 votes.

Ruto who was behind Bader, a former constituency development fund manager, said the win proved their faith in God and the voters who braved the scorching sun to cast their ballots.

"Congratulation my friend Feisal Bader. Your win cements our trust in God and the people. Democracy and people power have triumphed. May God bless Msambweni people. To others there's next time," he said.
