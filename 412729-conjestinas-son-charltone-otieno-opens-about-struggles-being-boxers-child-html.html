Conjestina's Son Charltone Otieno Opens up About Struggles of Being Boxer's Child

March 2024 · 3 minute read

- Conjestina's only child shared the plight of being the celebrity's only son and how he has picked up on the love for boxing from his mom

- Charltone Otieno also shared that his mother's health is still deteriorating and she is currently under his grandmother's care

- The young man is also pursuing boxing and even has his own club

Celebrated female boxer Conjestina Achieng's son has talked about the ups and downs of being the son of the award-winning fighter.

Charltone appeared on the afternoon show on Radio Jambo with the magnificent radio host Massawe Japanni where he laid bare what he deals with due to having a celebrity mother.

He opened up by saying that his mother is one of the most known female sports personalities in the country who needs no introduction.

"Sometimes you are walking on the streets in the estate and people think because I am Congestina's son, then I have money. But on the other hand, it gives me confidence when people say, like mother, like son, coz I can also box. This is something that is in my blood,'' he said.

He disclosed that since he was five years old, his mom started training him how to be a boxer, and through the craft, he met various celebrated fighters like Zarika.

Charltone also shared that his mum is mentally ill, and they had taken her to a hospital in Thika, and some people offered to help her after she gets the prescribed medication.

However, when she was discharged in October, those people who had pledged to help her were nowhere to be found, and so the family had no choice but to get her back home in the rural area.

With nothing to do and nowhere to go, her condition became worse. Currently, she is under the care of her mother, siblings and relatives.

Charltone is Conjestina's only child, and he shared that seeing his mother get sick really affected him as he was about to sit for his KCPE exams.

"Seeing her change really worked on my emotions and it interfered with my performance. I was also in a good private school and so her medication drained finances and so, I had to go to a nearby secondary school,'' he added.
