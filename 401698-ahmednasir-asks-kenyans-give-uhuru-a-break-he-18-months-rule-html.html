With less than 18 months to the next General Election, President Uhuru Kenyatta has been under pressure to deliver all pre-election pledges made by the Jubilee Party.

Senior counsel Ahmednasir Abdullahi however, has asked Kenyans to give the head of state a break noting he still has more time to deliver if he gets the correct advice.

Mixed reactions

His tweet sparked a heated debate on Twitter with some netizens noting most Kenyans were finding it hard to feed themselves and their families with businesses that offer jobs either closed or scaled down.

Here were some of the reactions:

Philiph Marube posed:

"He is not keen. Anger has taken a toll on him. His forceful push for Constitutional change is not genuine. There is something up the sleeves. What Will prevent him from running for presidency if the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) is passed? which law will bar him from running again?"

Ken Koech opined:

"Too late Sir, he had enough resources but unfortunately, couldn't perform. I think the only achievement we should expect is the BBI."
