Governor Waititu to get back KSh 15M paid as bail in exchange for surety

- The Kiambu governor was charged in connection to corruption involving KSh 588 million

- Waititu denied the allegations and was released on a KSh 15 million bail with alternative of a surety

- The county leader has now applied to the court seeking to have the money back in substitute of surety

Embattled Kiambu governor Ferdinand Waititu is set to receive KSh 15 million he posted as bail in an ongoing graft case after a former Nairobi mayor agreed to stand as his surety.

In a written application to the court which was agreed to by the prosecutor, Waititu cited financial constraints and he needed to use the money to meet his daily financial obligations.

He was charged alongside his wife Susan Ndung'u and the two were granted KSh 15 million and KSh 4 million bail respectively.

Waititu moved to court seeking to have the cash bail reduced but High Court Judge Ngenye Macharia turned down his application.

He deposited the money and was released after spending four nights in Industrial Area Police Station.
